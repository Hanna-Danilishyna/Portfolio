The Effect of Beauty Filters on Perceived Attractiveness

Investigated how different filter strengths affect subjective attractiveness perception and physiological responses (pupil dilation), providing insights into UX and social media design.

Study Design

  • Within-subject design: 16 participants, 3 filter intensities (0%, 100%, 200%)
  • AI-generated faces from thispersondoesnotexist.com
  • Participants rated faces on a 7-point scale while eye-tracking measured pupil responses

Visual Examples

Example Faces with Filters

Faces presented with 0%, 100%, and 200% filter intensities for participant evaluation.

Mean Attractiveness Ratings

Intermediate filter (100%) increased perceived attractiveness, 200% reduced trust and authenticity.

Key Insights

  • Intermediate filter levels increase perceived attractiveness
  • Overly strong filters reduce trustworthiness perception
  • Pupil dilation validates physiological measures of subjective ratings

Implications

  • Design choices affect user perception and self-image
  • Supports balanced filter design for social media UX

The Influence of Familiar vs. Unfamiliar Faces on Eyelid Distance

Studied how face familiarity affects eyelid distance and blink rate, exploring physiological indicators of emotion for UX research.

Study Design

  • Within-subject 2x3 design: 18 students, familiar vs. unfamiliar professor faces
  • Measured eyelid distance and blink rate using Tobii Pro Nano eye-tracker

Visual Data

Professors Shown

Images used to test recognition effects.

Eyelid Distance

Boxplot showing average eyelid distance across familiar and unfamiliar faces.

Blinking Rate

Boxplot showing blink rate differences by familiarity.

Key Findings

  • No significant change in eyelid distance based on familiarity
  • Blink rate slightly varied but overall consistent
  • Highlights complexity of using eyelid distance alone for emotion detection

Insights & Implications

  • Evidence for careful validation of biometric emotion detection
  • Supports future research on multimodal physiological signals in UX
