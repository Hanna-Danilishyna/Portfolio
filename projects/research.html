The Effect of Beauty Filters on Perceived Attractiveness

Investigated how different filter strengths affect subjective attractiveness and physiological responses (pupil dilation).

  • Within-subject design: 16 participants, 3 filter intensities (0%, 100%, 200%)
  • AI-generated faces from thispersondoesnotexist.com
  • Eye-tracking measured pupil dilation while participants rated faces

Example Faces with Filters

Participants saw faces with 0%, 100%, and 200% filter intensities.

Mean Attractiveness Ratings

Intermediate filter (100%) increased attractiveness; 200% decreased trust and authenticity.

Key Insights

  • Intermediate filter levels increase attractiveness perception
  • Overly strong filters reduce perceived trustworthiness
  • Pupil dilation validates physiological measure of subjective rating

The Influence of Familiar vs. Unfamiliar Faces on Eyelid Distance

Explored how face familiarity affects eyelid distance and blink rate to understand physiological indicators of emotion in UX research.

  • Within-subject 2x3 design, 18 students, familiar vs unfamiliar professor faces
  • Measured eyelid distance and blink rate using Tobii Pro Nano eye-tracker

Professors Shown

Images used in the experiment to test recognition effects.

Eyelid Distance

Boxplot showing average eyelid distance across familiar and unfamiliar faces.

Blinking Rate

Boxplot showing blink rate differences by familiarity.

Key Insights

  • No significant effect of familiarity on eyelid distance
  • Blink rate slightly varied but overall consistent
  • Highlights complexity of using eyelid distance alone for emotion detection
