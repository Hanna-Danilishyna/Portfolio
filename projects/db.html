Deutsche Bahn Passenger Experience

Contextual research and service design to improve passenger journeys, addressing real pain points and introducing actionable features.

Role: UX Researcher & Designer
Team: 5
Duration: 6 weeks
Tools: Figma · Paper prototyping · Contextual inquiry

Project Overview

Improving Deutsche Bahn passenger experience by uncovering real pain points and proposing actionable features. Focused on train cancellations, ticketing issues, and lack of scenic route notifications.

Goal

Understand passenger challenges through contextual design methods and propose actionable solutions for better journey experiences.

My Contribution

  • Conducted 5 contextual inquiries & journey observations
  • Facilitated interpretation sessions & affinity diagramming
  • Ran Wall Walk & Cool Drilldown workshops with stakeholders
  • Developed low-fidelity wireframes for top solutions

Problem

Passengers were frustrated by train cancellations, ticketing issues, lack of scenic route notifications, and poor real-time guidance during journeys.

Process Highlights

Contextual Inquiry

Observed 5 passengers during real journeys

Affinity Diagram

Grouped 100+ insights into key themes

Wall Walk & Co-Creation

Generated solutions for scenic routes and reminders

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Visualized top design solutions for testing

UX & Interface Design

Based on research insights, we designed new features to improve user experience, including reminders, scenic route notifications, and integration of student passes.

Reminder Feature

Sound notifications when the passenger approaches their destination.

Scenic Route Notification

Alerts passengers of interesting scenic views during journeys.

Comprehensive view of adding a student pass and accessing main features in the app.

Impact

  • 3 new features prioritized for future user testing
  • Demonstrated value of contextual research for transport UX
  • Improved passenger trust and satisfaction with Deutsche Bahn services
