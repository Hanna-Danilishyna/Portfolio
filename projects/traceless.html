TraceLess — The Overshared Self

A privacy analytics dashboard that visualizes personal digital exposure and transforms complex data into actionable insights.

Role: UX Researcher · Data Analyst · UX Designer
Duration: ~30 hours
Type: MSc Thesis Extension
Tools: Python (pandas, networkx, plotly) · Figma

Project Overview

TraceLess is a privacy analytics dashboard designed to help users understand and manage their digital exposure. The project investigates how fragmented pieces of publicly available information can be aggregated into a comprehensive personal exposure profile.

The work builds upon my master's thesis research on user awareness of personal data exposure, privacy behaviors, and the design of tools that support transparency and control over one's digital footprint.

Key Objectives

  • Quantify digital exposure across multiple data sources
  • Translate complex data into actionable privacy insights
  • Design an intuitive interface to communicate abstract risk information

Research & Insights

To understand how users perceive digital exposure, I conducted qualitative research combining interviews, surveys, and thematic analysis.

Research Methods

  • User interviews on awareness of publicly available data
  • Surveys exploring emotional responses to exposure
  • Thematic coding into structured variables:
  • Awareness Level
  • Surprise Level
  • Desire for Control
  • Emotional Reaction

Key Insights

  • Users consistently underestimated the scale of their public data
  • Awareness did not always translate into behavioral change
  • There was a strong demand for clear, visual privacy feedback

Quantitative Reinforcement

External datasets from social media and privacy studies were integrated to validate qualitative patterns and strengthen data reliability.

Personas

Based on qualitative interviews and thematic analysis, two key user types emerged.

The Anxious User

Wants to reduce digital footprint and regain control over personal data.

Goals

  • Reduce public exposure
  • Understand who can access their data
  • Regain privacy control

Frustrations

  • Overwhelmed by complex privacy settings
  • Does not know what data is public
  • Anxiety about misuse of personal info

The Conscious Analyst

Wants clear insights and measurable control over their digital presence.

Goals

  • Track exposure across platforms
  • Understand data aggregation risks
  • Make informed privacy decisions

Frustrations

  • Lack of transparency in existing tools
  • Difficult to quantify exposure
  • Manual monitoring is time-consuming

Data Model

The system translates raw public data into a structured exposure score through a multi-stage pipeline.

  • Data sources include social platforms, forums, and indexed content
  • Each data point is evaluated by sensitivity and visibility
  • Cross-linkability determines how easily data can be aggregated
  • All factors are combined into a normalized exposure score

Exposure Formula

Exposure Score = Sensitivity × Visibility × Cross-linkability

Network Visualization

A graph-based model reveals relationships between platforms, data points, and exposure risks.

Interface Design

The interface communicates complex privacy data through a clear three-level structure: overview, exploration, and detailed analysis.

Design Approach

The interface was designed to communicate complex privacy data through visual clarity, risk transparency, and structured information hierarchy.

  • Dark SaaS-style interface with deep navy background
  • Electric cyan accents for key interactions
  • Color-coded risk system: red, amber, green
  • Platform-specific colors for fast recognition
