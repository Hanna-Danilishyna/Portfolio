TraceLess — The Overshared Self

A privacy analytics dashboard that visualizes personal digital exposure and transforms complex data into actionable insights.

Role: UX Researcher · Data Analyst · Designer
Type: MSc Thesis Extension
Duration: ~30 hours

Project Overview

TraceLess explores how publicly available personal data can be aggregated into a measurable digital exposure profile. The goal was to create a system that quantifies risk while presenting it in an intuitive and transparent way.

Research Insights

  • Users underestimate the scale of publicly available personal data
  • Strong desire for transparency and control
  • Existing tools lack clarity and actionable insights

Personas

Based on qualitative interviews and thematic analysis, two key user types emerged.

The Anxious User

Wants to reduce digital footprint and regain control over personal data.

Goals

  • Reduce public exposure
  • Understand who can access their data
  • Regain privacy control

Frustrations

  • Overwhelmed by complex privacy settings
  • Does not know what data is public
  • Anxiety about misuse of personal info

The Conscious Analyst

Wants clear insights and measurable control over their digital presence.

Goals

  • Track exposure across platforms
  • Understand data aggregation risks
  • Make informed privacy decisions

Frustrations

  • Lack of transparency in existing tools
  • Difficult to quantify exposure
  • Manual monitoring is time-consuming

Data Model

The system models digital exposure through a structured pipeline that transforms raw public data into a measurable exposure score.

  • Data sources include social platforms, forums, and indexed content
  • Each data point is evaluated by sensitivity and visibility
  • Cross-linkability determines how easily data can be aggregated
  • All factors are combined into a normalized exposure score

Exposure Formula

Exposure Score = Sensitivity × Visibility × Cross-linkability

Network Visualization

A graph-based model reveals relationships between platforms, data points, and exposure risks.

Interface Design

The interface communicates complex privacy data through a clear three-level structure: overview, exploration, and detailed analysis.
